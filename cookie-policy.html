Cookie Policy

What cookies this website uses and how to control them.

Effective date: 1 June 2026  ·  Last updated: 1 June 2026

1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by a website when you visit it. They are widely used to make websites work, remember your preferences, and provide information to site owners.

Other similar technologies — such as local storage, session storage, and pixel tags — serve comparable purposes and are covered by this policy.

2. Our Approach

We have designed this website to use as few cookies and third-party requests as possible. This website uses analytics and visitor intelligence tools in addition to those necessary to load fonts and render embedded forms. We do not use behavioural advertising networks. All third-party services used on this website are described below.

3. Cookies and Third-Party Requests on This Website

3.1 Strictly Necessary

This website itself does not set any first-party cookies. No login, session, or preference cookies are used.

3.2 Google Fonts

This website loads typefaces from Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com and fonts.gstatic.com). When your browser fetches these font files, it sends a network request to Google's servers. Google may collect your IP address and browser information as part of this request.

Google states that it does not use data collected via Google Fonts to build advertising profiles or serve targeted ads. See Google Fonts Privacy FAQ and Google's Privacy Policy.

Google Fonts requests do not set persistent cookies on your device.

3.3 Zoho Forms

Forms on this website are embedded using Zoho Forms iFrames. Zoho may serve these from its own domain or a custom domain configured by HearITright in order to:

  • Maintain your form session while you fill it in
  • Prevent duplicate submissions
  • Ensure form security (CSRF protection)

These cookies are set by Zoho's domain, not by hearitright.com. They are strictly necessary for the forms to function and are not used for tracking or advertising. See Zoho's Privacy Policy for full details.

3.4 GitHub Pages (Hosting)

This website is hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub may process standard HTTP request data (IP address, browser type, referring page) as part of serving the website. GitHub does not set tracking cookies through this website. See GitHub's Privacy Statement.

3.5 Google Analytics

This website uses Google Analytics to collect anonymised data about how visitors use the site — including pages visited, time on site, browser type, device type, and approximate location derived from IP address. IP anonymisation is enabled. Google Analytics sets persistent cookies on your device to distinguish returning visitors and sessions. See Google's Privacy Policy for details. You may opt out using Google's opt-out browser add-on.

3.6 Zoho SalesIQ

This website uses Zoho SalesIQ for visitor intelligence and live chat. SalesIQ may set cookies to identify returning visitors, track pages viewed during your session, and enable the live chat interface. If you initiate a chat, any information you provide will be collected and stored by Zoho. See Zoho's Privacy Policy for full details.

3.7 Future Tools

We may add further third-party tools — including advertising or analytics platforms such as Meta Pixel — over time. This policy will be updated when such tools are added. The summary table in Section 4 will reflect all active services at the time of the last update date shown above.

5. How to Control Cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. The links below explain how to do this in the most common browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Please note that blocking all cookies may affect the functionality of embedded forms on this website. It will not affect your ability to read or navigate the rest of the site.

To opt out of Google's data collection across the web, you may use Google's opt-out tools.

6. Do Not Track

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) setting. This website uses analytics and visitor intelligence tools that may not respond to DNT signals. You can manage your preferences through your browser cookie settings or the opt-out tools provided by each service described in Section 3.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy if we add new features or third-party services to this website. Any changes will be reflected with an updated "Last updated" date. We recommend reviewing this page periodically.
